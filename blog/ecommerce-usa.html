Why E-Commerce in the USA Is a Game-Changer for Global Entrepreneurs

April 1, 2026·8 min read·by AppTechno
🇺🇸

If you're a global entrepreneur looking for the most powerful market to enter in 2026, the answer is almost always the same: the United States. With over 270 million digital shoppers, a payment infrastructure that supports merchants from 190+ countries, and a legal framework built for business agility, the US e-commerce landscape is unmatched anywhere on Earth.

But here's what most guides don't tell you: you don't have to be in the USA to operate a US e-commerce business. With the right structure in place, you can run a fully legitimate American business from Stockholm, Istanbul, or Singapore — paying US-compliant taxes, accepting USD payments, and building trust with American consumers who prefer to buy from US-registered companies.

The Scale Advantage: Why the US Market Is Incomparable

The US e-commerce market surpassed $1.1 trillion in annual sales in 2024, and projections for 2026 look even stronger. Compare this to the EU's fragmented regulatory landscape or Asia's steep platform localisation costs, and the case for a US-first strategy becomes compelling.

  • Consumer spending power: The average American spends over $5,000 per year online — nearly double the EU average.
  • Platform access: Selling on Amazon US, Shopify, Walmart Marketplace, and eBay gives you access to the world's most active buyer base.
  • Payment simplicity: Stripe, PayPal, and Square all work seamlessly for US-registered businesses, offering instant payouts and low fraud rates.
  • Credibility signal: A .com domain and a US business address immediately increase conversion rates with American shoppers.

What You Actually Need to Start: The US LLC

The most common and cost-effective legal structure for international e-commerce entrepreneurs entering the US market is the Limited Liability Company (LLC). An LLC gives you:

  • A legal US business entity that can open US bank accounts
  • An Employer Identification Number (EIN) required for payment processors
  • Pass-through taxation — meaning profits flow directly to you without corporate-level US tax (for non-US residents in most cases)
  • Limited personal liability: your personal assets are protected from business debts
  • A professional business address and registered agent in the state of formation

Popular states for LLC formation include Wyoming (lowest fees, strongest privacy protections), Delaware (preferred for VC-backed startups), and Florida (no state income tax). For most e-commerce businesses with no physical US presence, Wyoming is the optimal choice due to its low annual fees and minimal reporting requirements.

The Accounting Side: Don't Get It Wrong

This is where many international entrepreneurs trip up. US accounting and tax compliance is not the same as in the EU or elsewhere. Key obligations include:

  • Sales Tax nexus: After South Dakota v. Wayfair (2018), online sellers may owe sales tax in states where they exceed certain revenue thresholds — even without a physical presence. This varies by state.
  • Annual reporting: Most states require LLCs to file an annual report and pay a state fee to keep the entity in good standing.
  • EIN and ITIN: You'll need an EIN (Employer Identification Number) from the IRS for your LLC. Non-US residents may also need an ITIN for individual tax filings.
  • Form 5472: Foreign-owned single-member LLCs must file Form 5472 with the IRS each year — failure results in a $25,000 penalty.
  • FBAR: If you have US bank accounts with balances exceeding $10,000 at any point during the year, you may need to file an FBAR.
Getting US tax compliance right from day one is far cheaper than cleaning up a mess later. The rules are navigable — but you need the right guidance.

E-Commerce Platforms: Which One to Choose

Shopify

The gold standard for direct-to-consumer (DTC) brands. Shopify gives you full control over your storefront, customer data, and branding. With a US LLC and a US bank account, you can accept Shopify Payments (the lowest-fee option) and access Shopify Capital for financing.

Amazon FBA

Fulfillment by Amazon is ideal for physical product sellers. Amazon handles storage, shipping, and customer service. Your US LLC gives you access to Amazon's North American marketplace. Note: you'll need to comply with Amazon's seller compliance requirements and may need a US-based product liability insurance policy.

Etsy

For handmade, vintage, or creative products, Etsy's US buyer base is enormous. A US LLC and US payment details increase trust and conversion rates on the platform.

Your Own Website

For scaling and margin optimization, owning your customer relationship is essential. A US LLC lets you integrate Stripe US (lower processing fees for USD transactions) and build long-term brand equity.

The US e-commerce opportunity has never been more accessible for international entrepreneurs. The combination of a favourable LLC structure, mature payment infrastructure, and the scale of the American consumer market creates a powerful foundation for any digital business.

The complexity lies not in the idea — but in the legal, financial, and administrative setup that makes it sustainable and compliant. If you're ready to take the leap, start with your LLC formation, get your financial tracking in place, and build from there.
