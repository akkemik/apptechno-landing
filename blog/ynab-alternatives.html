7 Best YNAB Alternatives in 2026 (Free & Cheaper Options)

April 21, 2026 · 8 min read · by AppTechno

YNAB (You Need A Budget) has long been the gold standard of personal finance apps — and for good reason. Its zero-based budgeting philosophy genuinely works. But in 2024 the company raised its annual price to $109/year (up from $99), and the monthly plan sits at $14.99/month. For many users, especially those in Europe or on tighter budgets, that's a hard number to justify.

The good news: the budgeting app market has never been more competitive. Several excellent alternatives now offer YNAB-like features — and some are completely free. This guide covers the 7 best YNAB alternatives in 2026, with honest pros, cons, and who each one is best for.

2. Goodbudget — Best for the Envelope Method

✉️
Goodbudget
Free plan available · $10/month for Plus

Goodbudget brings the traditional envelope budgeting method to digital form. You allocate money into virtual "envelopes" (categories) at the start of each month — the same foundational concept that YNAB is built on. It's particularly well-suited for couples who want to budget together.

  • Excellent free plan (10 envelopes, 1 account, 2 devices)
  • Genuine envelope method — intuitive for YNAB refugees
  • Shared household budgeting — sync across devices for couples
  • Available on iOS and Android
  • No automatic bank sync on free plan
  • UI feels dated compared to newer apps
  • Limited to USD-centric design

Best for: Couples and households who specifically want envelope budgeting and don't need bank sync. The free plan is genuinely useful.

3. EveryDollar — Best for Dave Ramsey's Baby Steps

💵
EveryDollar
Free plan available · $17.99/month for Premium

Built by Ramsey Solutions, EveryDollar is designed around zero-based budgeting and Dave Ramsey's 7 Baby Steps financial framework. The free plan is a functional manual budgeting tool. The premium version adds bank sync and debt payoff tracking.

  • Clean, simple interface — easy for budgeting beginners
  • True zero-based budgeting
  • Debt payoff tracker built in (Baby Steps alignment)
  • Free plan is fully functional for manual entry users
  • Bank sync requires expensive Premium plan ($17.99/month)
  • Heavily US-centric (no multi-currency)
  • Tied to Ramsey's financial philosophy — not for everyone

Best for: US users following Dave Ramsey's debt payoff method who want a free zero-based budgeting app.

4. Empower Personal Dashboard — Best Free All-in-One

📊
Empower Personal Dashboard
Completely free (formerly Personal Capital)

Empower (formerly Personal Capital) offers the most comprehensive free financial dashboard available — connecting bank accounts, investment portfolios, retirement accounts, and loans in one view. Its budgeting features are less granular than YNAB, but the investment tracking is unmatched in the free tier.

  • 100% free — no paywall on core features
  • Automatic bank and investment account sync
  • Net worth tracking across all accounts
  • Retirement planner and investment fee analyser
  • Excellent spending category charts
  • Not a zero-based budgeting tool — more of a tracker
  • Wealth management upsells can feel intrusive
  • US-only for bank sync

Best for: US users who want automatic bank sync and investment tracking for free, and don't need strict envelope/zero-based budgeting.

5. Monarch Money — Best Mint Replacement

👑
Monarch Money
$99/year (7-day free trial)

After Mint shut down in 2024, Monarch Money became the top destination for former Mint users. It offers automatic bank sync, customisable budgets, net worth tracking, and shared household features. At $99/year it's cheaper than YNAB but still a premium product.

  • Polished, modern UI — the cleanest budgeting app available
  • Automatic bank sync with 11,000+ institutions
  • Collaborative budgeting for couples and households
  • Custom categories and rules for automatic transaction sorting
  • Net worth and investment tracking included
  • $99/year — not free
  • US and Canada only for bank sync
  • No zero-based budgeting framework (category-based only)

Best for: Former Mint users looking for a polished, full-featured replacement who are willing to pay $99/year.

6. Copilot — Best for Apple Users

✈️
Copilot Money
$95/year (free trial available)

Copilot is an iOS and macOS-exclusive budgeting app that leverages Apple's design language for an exceptionally polished experience. It uses machine learning to automatically categorise transactions and offers flexible budgeting frameworks — not strictly zero-based but highly customisable.

  • Best-in-class UI/UX — feels native on iPhone and Mac
  • Smart transaction categorisation with ML
  • Flexible spending plan (not locked to zero-based)
  • Automatic bank sync
  • iOS/macOS only — no Android, no web
  • US-only bank sync
  • $95/year — not cheap

Best for: Apple ecosystem users in the US who prioritise design and are willing to pay for a premium experience.

7. Money Manager Ex — Best Offline / Privacy-First Option

🔒
Money Manager Ex (MMEX)
Free and open source

Money Manager Ex is a free, open-source desktop budgeting application that stores all data locally. It has no cloud sync, no subscriptions, and no data collection. For users who want maximum privacy and offline functionality, MMEX is the most trustworthy option available — though it requires a steeper setup.

  • 100% free and open source
  • All data stored locally — no cloud, no data sharing
  • Multi-currency and multi-language support
  • Available on Windows, macOS, Linux, and Android
  • Import/export CSV and QIF formats
  • Steep learning curve — not beginner-friendly
  • No automatic bank sync by design
  • UI looks dated

Best for: Privacy-conscious users, European users uncomfortable with US-based cloud services, and power users who want full control over their financial data.

Which YNAB Alternative Should You Choose?

The right alternative depends on your priorities:

  • Price matters most: BudgetWise (free), Goodbudget (free tier), or Money Manager Ex (free, open source)
  • You're outside the US: BudgetWise (multi-currency), Money Manager Ex
  • You need automatic bank sync: Empower (free, US only), Monarch Money ($99/yr), Copilot ($95/yr, Apple only)
  • Envelope method purist: Goodbudget or EveryDollar
  • Couples budgeting: Goodbudget or Monarch Money
  • Former Mint user: Monarch Money
The best budgeting app is the one you'll actually use. A free app you stick with beats an expensive one you abandon after two months.

Why People Are Leaving YNAB

YNAB remains excellent software, but several trends are pushing users toward alternatives:

  • Price increases: From $6.99/month in 2015 to $14.99/month in 2026 — a 114% increase
  • No permanent free tier: The 34-day trial ends and payment is required
  • US-centric bank sync: International users get limited bank connection support
  • Complexity: YNAB's learning curve is steep — some users want simpler tools
  • Privacy concerns: Cloud-stored financial data concerns some users

None of these are fatal flaws — but they mean that for a significant portion of YNAB's user base, a cheaper or free alternative covers their actual needs.

FAQ

Does YNAB have a free version?

No. YNAB offers a 34-day free trial. After that, the price is $14.99/month or $109/year. Students with a valid .edu email get one free year.

Is there a YNAB alternative that works in Europe?

BudgetWise is the best option for European users — it supports multi-currency budgeting and doesn't require a US bank account. Money Manager Ex also works globally since it doesn't connect to banks at all.

What happened to Mint?

Intuit shut down Mint in January 2024, redirecting users to Credit Karma. Most former Mint users have migrated to Monarch Money, Empower, or YNAB. BudgetWise is also a strong option for former Mint users who want free budgeting.

Can I import my YNAB data into another app?

Yes. YNAB allows you to export your transactions as a CSV file. Most alternatives, including BudgetWise, Monarch Money, and MMEX, support CSV import so you can bring your history with you.
